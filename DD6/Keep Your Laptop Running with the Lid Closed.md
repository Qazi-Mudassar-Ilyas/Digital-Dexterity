# Keep Your Laptop Running with the Lid Closed
Do you carry your laptop with the lid open because you're worried it will go to sleep when you close it? Besides being inconvenient, carrying an open laptop increases the risk of accidental drops, screen damage, and keyboard stress.
Windows allows you to choose what happens when you close the laptop lid. You can configure it to *do nothing*, allowing the laptop to continue running while the lid is closed. This is useful when moving between meeting rooms, giving presentations, downloading large files, or connecting to an external monitor.
## To change this setting:
1.	Open Control Panel.
2.	Select Power Options.
3.	Click Choose what closing the lid does.
4.	Under When I close the lid, select Do nothing for On battery, Plugged in, or both.
5.	Click Save changes.
## Important: 
This setting should be used only when you intend to keep the laptop running. If you place a running laptop inside a tightly enclosed bag for an extended period, heat can build up and affect performance or battery life. When carrying your laptop in a bag for long periods, it is generally better to put it to Sleep, Hibernate, or Shut down.
