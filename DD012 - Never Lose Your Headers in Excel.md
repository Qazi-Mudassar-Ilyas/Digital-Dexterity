# Never Lose Your Headers in Excel

## The Idea
Freeze rows and columns in Excel to keep important information, such as headers or labels, visible while scrolling through large worksheets.

## Why It Matters
When working with long lists or wide tables, column headings and row labels quickly disappear from view. Freezing panes keeps key information visible, making it easier to read, compare, and enter data accurately without constantly scrolling back.

## Try It
## To freeze the top row:
• Go to the View tab.
• Click Freeze Panes.
• Select Freeze Top Row.

## To freeze the first column:
• Go to View → Freeze Panes.
• Select Freeze First Column.

## To freeze both rows and columns:
• Select the cell immediately below the row(s) and to the right of the column(s) you want to keep visible.
• Go to View → Freeze Panes → Freeze Panes.
For example, selecting cell B2 freezes Row 1 and Column A. Selecting C3 freezes Rows 1–2 and Columns A–B.

## Pro Tip
Need to remove the frozen panes? Go to View → Freeze Panes → Unfreeze Panes. If you frequently analyze large datasets, freezing panes should be one of the first things you do after opening a worksheet.

## Works On
Microsoft Excel for Windows, Mac, and Excel for the Web (with minor interface differences).
